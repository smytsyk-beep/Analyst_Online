# Analyst Online — Core Project Rules

Updated project rules after repositioning Analyst Online and reducing OmniDash from flagship product to one service direction.

---

## 1. Project identity

This is one unified commercial project.

- **Analyst Online** = umbrella brand, SEO website, lead generation platform, trust layer, and commercial service catalog.
- **Main positioning** = AI, analytics, and automation for business.
- **OmniDash** = one service/product page inside Analyst Online focused on e-commerce dashboards, but **not the flagship product anymore**.

Main business goal = build a system that can generate **€1000–€2000/month** through:

- AI / ChatGPT training for businesses and employees,
- AI implementation and workflow automation,
- Google Sheets / Excel / Google Apps Script automation,
- dashboards and reporting,
- analytics consulting and audits,
- part-time / outstaff analyst services,
- OmniDash / e-commerce dashboards as a secondary productized offer.

Do not treat Analyst Online and OmniDash as two unrelated projects.

Correct model:

- Analyst Online attracts and warms up leads,
- AI / analytics / automation services monetize the traffic,
- outstaff analyst services create higher-value recurring revenue,
- OmniDash remains a specialized offer for e-commerce clients,
- all service directions should support cross-sell and upsell.

---

## 2. Product strategy

### Analyst Online

Analyst Online is:

- the main brand,
- the public website,
- SEO and content platform,
- service catalog,
- lead generation platform,
- entry point for all service inquiries.

Main homepage positioning:

> **AI, аналитика и автоматизация для бизнеса**

The website should communicate that Analyst Online helps small businesses and teams:

- learn and implement AI tools,
- automate routine workflows,
- build reporting systems,
- work with Google Sheets / Excel / Apps Script,
- create dashboards and analytics processes,
- get an external analyst on a part-time or project basis.

### OmniDash

OmniDash is:

- a secondary productized service inside Analyst Online,
- a dashboard/reporting offer for e-commerce businesses,
- a possible upsell for clients who need structured sales, ads, and profitability analytics.

OmniDash is **not**:

- the flagship product,
- the main site positioning,
- the main navigation focus,
- the only scalable monetization direction.

Main rule:

- Keep `/omnidash` as a public service/product page.
- Remove “flagship” language from homepage, services, navigation, and assistant rules.
- Present OmniDash as “dashboards for e-commerce” or “e-commerce analytics dashboards”.

Reason for repositioning:

- Google and analytics platforms continue improving native integrations and ad-cost import options.
- Pure dashboard-as-a-service is less defensible as the main flagship direction.
- The stronger positioning is broader: **AI + analytics + automation + business support**.

## 5. Service hierarchy

### Priority services for the first implementation stage

1. **AI / ChatGPT training for business**
   - individual lessons,
   - team training,
   - AI usage for daily work,
   - prompt writing,
   - AI for documents, emails, analysis, and decisions.

2. **AI for business and employees**
   - practical adoption of AI in company workflows,
   - AI tools for managers, sales, marketing, finance, HR, and operations,
   - employee enablement and internal AI playbooks.

3. **Business process automation with AI**
   - reducing manual work,
   - automated reports,
   - Telegram/email notifications,
   - document generation,
   - internal workflow simplification.

4. **AI + Google Sheets / Excel**
   - formulas,
   - data cleaning,
   - report templates,
   - table automation,
   - simple management reporting,
   - preparation for Apps Script automation.

5. **Google Sheets / Apps Script automation**
   - custom scripts,
   - scheduled imports,
   - reports,
   - email/Telegram notifications,
   - document and table workflows.

6. **Dashboards and reports**
   - Looker Studio,
   - Power BI,
   - Google Sheets dashboards,
   - executive reporting,
   - marketing/sales/finance reports.

7. **Analyst outstaffing / part-time analyst**
   - external analyst for small business,
   - project-based analytics support,
   - regular reporting and analysis,
   - data audit and dashboard maintenance,
   - serious high-value recurring service direction.

8. **OmniDash / e-commerce dashboards**
   - specialized dashboard service for e-commerce,
   - ads + sales + profit visibility,
   - secondary productized offer, not the flagship.

### First-stage exclusion

Do **not** make n8n / Make.com a separate first-stage service page.

Allowed approach:

- mention n8n / Make.com only as optional tools inside automation content,
- do not put them into the main navigation,
- do not create a separate SEO page for them until the core AI cluster is implemented.

12. **Referral channel**

## 15. Development principles

Always optimize for:

- simplicity,
- maintainability in 3–6 months,
- modular structure,
- explicit logic,
- minimal diffs,
- easy content editing,
- scalable lead generation.

Prefer:

- simple architecture over clever architecture,
- reusable sections over one-off hacks,
- clear naming over abstraction,
- modularity over hidden magic.

Do not rewrite large files unless necessary.

When changing code:

- show focused diffs,
- explain only important decisions,
- note possible risks or side effects.
